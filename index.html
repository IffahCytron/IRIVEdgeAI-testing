Frequently Asked Questions

What is TOPS and how do I choose between 13 TOPS and 26 TOPS? ▼
TOPS (Tera Operations Per Second) measures AI inference performance. 13 TOPS is ideal for pilot projects, standard models, and fewer concurrent camera streams. Choose 26 TOPS for production deployments, complex AI models, and multiple simultaneous camera feeds. If you're unsure, start with 13 TOPS for evaluation and scale up as needed.
Does it support multi-camera setups? ▼
Yes, the IRIV EdgeAI CM5 supports multiple CSI cameras simultaneously. The number of concurrent streams depends on your AI model complexity and the TOPS variant you choose. The 26 TOPS variant can handle more concurrent camera feeds than the 13 TOPS version.
Can it run offline without internet connectivity? ▼
Absolutely. The IRIV EdgeAI CM5 is designed for edge computing, meaning all AI inference happens locally on the device. It operates completely offline once your models are deployed. Internet connectivity is only needed for initial setup, model updates, or if you choose to send data to cloud services.
What's the typical deployment workflow? ▼
Typical workflow: 1) Unbox and mount the device, 2) Connect cameras and network, 3) Flash the OS image, 4) Deploy your AI models (or use pre-trained samples), 5) Configure inference parameters, 6) Integrate with your systems (MQTT/Node-RED/REST), 7) Monitor and optimize. Our Quick Start Guide covers each step in detail.
Can I integrate with MQTT, Node-RED, or REST APIs? ▼
Yes, the IRIV EdgeAI CM5 supports standard industrial protocols. You can publish inference results via MQTT, create automation workflows in Node-RED, or expose REST APIs for integration with your existing systems. The SDK includes examples and libraries for all these integration methods.
